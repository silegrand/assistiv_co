Assistiv Systems · Platform overview

Helping older people stay
at home, safely and independently.

Right now, there are 3.5 million older people in England who are neither fully independent nor in formal care. They are struggling quietly — and nobody is watching. Assistiv exists to find them, support them, and keep them safe — before a crisis that could have been prevented.

The problem

There is a gap between
independence and care —
and it is where people fall.

Most older people do not suddenly become frail overnight. They decline gradually — slowly needing more help, slowly becoming less safe. But the system only really notices when something goes wrong: a fall, a hospital admission, a crisis. By then, the damage is done, the cost is high, and the person's independence may be gone for good.

The Missing Middle

We call this group the Missing Middle. They are too well to qualify for formal social care. They are too frail to manage entirely on their own. They are invisible to the system — until the moment they aren't. That moment costs the NHS an average of £12,000 per emergency admission. More importantly, it costs the person their confidence, their independence, and often their home.

Our approach

Find them early. Support them
at home. Before crisis arrives.

Assistiv works in three stages. First, we identify people who are at risk — using data that already exists. Second, we screen and connect those people with the right community support. Third, we provide a home monitoring and care service that keeps them safe, connected and independent on their own terms. Each stage connects to the next.

Stage one
Find
Identify people in the Missing Middle using NHS and population data — before they reach crisis.
→
Stage two
Screen
Meet people in the community — at pharmacies, GP surgeries, libraries — and connect them with support.
→
Stage three
Support
Provide passive home sensing, a family connection app, a unified care record, and a 24/7 staffed response centre.

The three platforms

One mission.
Three connected tools.

Each Assistiv platform does a distinct job. Together they form a complete picture — from identifying someone at risk, to supporting them at home for years to come. Here is what each one does, who it is for, and how it connects to the others.

assistiv.cloud
Population Intelligence
Finding the Missing Middle — before crisis
For NHS & commissioners

Assistiv Cloud is a tool for NHS teams and local health planners. It analyses publicly available health data across Kent and Medway to identify which areas — and which communities — have the highest concentration of people in the Missing Middle. Think of it as a map that shows where the need is greatest, so that the right resources can be sent to the right places before people hit crisis point. No individual is identified. The tool works at a neighbourhood level, helping decision-makers understand population risk rather than individual cases.

📍 District-level risk maps 📊 NHS open data 🔄 Daily updates 🏥 NHS pressure intelligence
Visit assistiv.cloud →
assistiv.tools
Community Screening
Meeting people where they are
For clinicians & community workers

Once assistiv.cloud has identified which areas need attention, assistiv.tools helps community workers, pharmacists, GPs and volunteers find and support individual people. The tools include voice-first frailty screening — a conversational check that feels like a friendly chat, not a medical assessment — and a carer companion app for families supporting an older relative. When someone is identified as being in the Missing Middle, assistiv.tools points them towards the right support — whether that is a community group, a GP referral, or the Assistiv Services platform.

🎙 Voice-first screening 🗺 Community outreach map 👨‍👩‍👧 Carer companion 📋 GP referral output
Visit assistiv.tools →
assistiv.services
Home Support Platform
Living safely at home, for longer
For individuals & families

Assistiv Services is what the older person and their family actually experience. It has four parts that work together. Home Sensing — small, invisible devices installed at home that detect falls, monitor routines and pick up early signs of change, all without cameras or wearables. Inner Circle — a private app connecting the older person with their family, where the person controls what is shared. Unified Care Record — NHS appointments, care plans and community services, all in one place. Assistiv Response Centre — a real person available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, who responds when the sensors suggest something is wrong.

How it all connects

One person's journey through
the whole system.

The three platforms are not separate products. They are three stages of the same story. Here is how they connect in practice — following one person, Margaret, aged 77, living alone in Faversham, Kent.

1
Margaret is identified as being at risk
The NHS Faversham PCN uses assistiv.cloud to review frailty risk across their area. The data shows that Margaret's neighbourhood has a high concentration of older people living alone with signs of emerging frailty. The PCN decides to run a community outreach programme in that area.
assistiv.cloud
2
Margaret is screened at her local pharmacy
A community health worker visits Faversham pharmacy, equipped with the assistiv.tools voice-first screening tool. Margaret comes in to collect her prescription. The conversation takes ten minutes and feels like a chat. The tool identifies that she is in the Missing Middle — managing, but not comfortably. She is offered a referral to Assistiv Services and agrees.
assistiv.tools
3
Margaret's home is set up — on her terms
An Assistiv advisor visits Margaret at home and has a "Design Your Life" conversation — asking what matters most to her. Margaret says she worries about falling at night, and that her daughter Sarah worries too. In response, she agrees to a small radar sensor on her bedroom ceiling and an energy monitor on her electricity supply. Nothing else. She sets up Inner Circle and invites Sarah. She chooses to share her morning routine status — nothing more.
assistiv.services
4
Life goes on — safely
Three months later, the sensor detects that Margaret has not moved from her bedroom for an unusual length of time at 6am. The Assistiv Response Centre calls her. She had simply overslept. She is fine. Sarah receives a gentle message — "Mum's morning looked a little different today — all confirmed well." Nobody panicked. Nobody was hospitalised. Margaret is still at home.

Our commitments

Independent living,
with dignity intact.

Assistiv is built on a simple belief: older people should be able to live at home, safely, for as long as they choose — not until the system decides otherwise. Every part of the platform reflects three non-negotiable commitments.

🔒
Privacy first
No cameras. No voice recordings. All sensor data is processed inside the home. Only derived insights — not raw data — ever leave the four walls. The person owns their data.
✋
Person in control
The older person decides what sensors go in, what their family can see, and what support they receive. Nothing is done to them. Everything is done with them.
🌱
Prevention over reaction
The whole point of Assistiv is to act before crisis. A quiet signal detected early is worth infinitely more than an emergency response that comes too late.
